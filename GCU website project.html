Why is binary search O(log n) and not O(n)?
14 students
▸ Show AI answer
Each step halves the search space. For n=1024 items, only 10 steps needed (log₂1024=10). That's O(log n) not O(n) — steps grow with the logarithm of n, not with n itself.
What is the difference between stack and queue?
9 students
▸ Show AI answer
Stack = LIFO (Last In First Out) — like a pile of plates, last placed is first removed, uses push() and pop(). Queue = FIFO (First In First Out) — like a ticket counter, first in is first served, uses enqueue() and dequeue().
Can binary search work on an unsorted array?
7 students
▸ Show AI answer
No — binary search requires a sorted array. It works by comparing the middle element and eliminating half the elements. On unsorted data this logic completely breaks down.
